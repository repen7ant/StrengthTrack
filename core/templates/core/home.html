Why smart planning beats training at maximum every session

Natural athletes have limited recovery resources. Training to failure or near-max effort every session leads to accumulated fatigue, stalled progress, and increased injury risk. Long-term strength gains require managing fatigue, not ignoring it.

Periodization is the structured variation of training load, intensity, and volume over time. Instead of pushing hard every week, you alternate between accumulation, intensification, peak, and recovery phases. This allows consistent progress while controlling fatigue.

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) and RIR (Reps in Reserve) help adjust training loads based on how hard a set actually feels. This autoregulation accounts for daily fluctuations in recovery, sleep, stress, and readiness — something fixed percentages cannot do.

StrengthTrack automatically generates mesocycles, tracks estimated 1RM progression, and helps plan loads using RPE/RIR principles. The goal is sustainable strength gains — not short-term burnout.
